Scheduling in Factory Flow allows you to define work shifts, assign them to machines, and use shift-based analytics to understand performance during specific time periods.

## Understanding shifts

A shift represents a defined work period when production is expected to occur. Shifts help you:

- **Calculate accurate OEE** - Know when machines should be running
- **Plan capacity** - Understand available production hours
- **Analyze by time period** - Compare performance across shifts
- **Manage staffing** - Align data with crew schedules

## Creating a shift

To create a new shift:

1. Navigate to **Shifts** in the sidebar
2. Click **Create Shift**
3. Configure the shift:

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Name** | Descriptive name (e.g., "Day Shift", "Night Shift") |
| **Schedule** | Days and times the shift runs |

4. Click **Create**

### Configuring the schedule

Define when the shift runs:

1. **Select days** - Choose which days of the week
2. **Set start time** - When the shift begins
3. **Set end time** - When the shift ends
4. **Handle overnight** - Shifts can cross midnight

### Example shift configurations

| Shift Name | Days | Start | End |
|------------|------|-------|-----|
| Day Shift | Mon-Fri | 6:00 AM | 2:00 PM |
| Swing Shift | Mon-Fri | 2:00 PM | 10:00 PM |
| Night Shift | Mon-Fri | 10:00 PM | 6:00 AM |
| Weekend Day | Sat-Sun | 7:00 AM | 7:00 PM |

## Managing shifts

### Viewing shifts

The Shifts page displays all shifts in your organization:

- Shift name
- Schedule summary
- Number of machines assigned
- Last updated

### Editing shifts

1. Click on a shift to open its detail page
2. Modify the name or schedule
3. Click **Save**

### Deleting shifts

1. Navigate to the shift's detail page
2. Click **Delete**
3. Confirm the deletion

<Warning>
  Deleting a shift disassociates it from all machines. Analytics history is preserved but future calculations won't use this shift.
</Warning>

## Assigning shifts to machines

Connect shifts to machines for accurate analytics:

### From the shift page

1. Open the shift detail page
2. Navigate to the **Machines** section
3. Click **Add Machines**
4. Select machines from the list
5. Click **Save**

### From the machine page

1. Open a machine's detail page
2. Find the **Shift** field
3. Select a shift from the dropdown
4. Click **Save**

### Multiple shifts per machine

If a machine runs during multiple shifts:
- Assign the shift that covers the most production time
- Or create a combined shift for continuous operations

## Shift-based analytics

Shifts enable powerful time-based analysis.

### Filtering by shift

In the Analytics dashboard:

1. Navigate to **Analytics**
2. Select a machine or line
3. Use the **Shift** filter
4. Choose a specific shift and date

### Shift metrics

Compare performance across shifts:

| Metric | Insight |
|--------|---------|
| **OEE by shift** | Which crew performs best? |
| **Throughput by shift** | Volume differences |
| **Downtime by shift** | When do problems occur? |
| **Quality by shift** | Defect patterns |

### Time window options

Analytics support various time selections:

- **Last N hours** - Recent performance (1, 8, 24 hours)
- **Last N days** - Short-term trends (7, 30, 90 days)
- **By shift and date** - Specific shift on a specific day
- **Custom range** - Any date range you specify

## Capacity planning with shifts

Use shifts to calculate production capacity.

### Available hours

Calculate weekly capacity:

```
Available Hours = Sum of (Shift Duration × Days per Week)
```

Example:
- Day Shift: 8 hours × 5 days = 40 hours
- Night Shift: 8 hours × 5 days = 40 hours
- Total: 80 hours per week

### Planned vs actual

Compare shift schedules to actual production:

- **Planned hours** - Based on shift definitions
- **Actual production hours** - Based on data received
- **Utilization** - Actual ÷ Planned

### Identifying gaps

Find opportunities to increase output:

- Machines idle during shifts
- Unscheduled downtime
- Extended changeovers
- Understaffed periods

## Best practices

### Shift naming

Use clear, consistent names:

| Good | Why |
|------|-----|
| "A Shift (6 AM - 2 PM)" | Clear times |
| "Day Shift - Building 1" | Location specific |
| "Weekend Crew" | Self-explanatory |

### Schedule accuracy

- **Match reality** - Shifts should reflect actual work times
- **Include breaks** - Decide if breaks are in or out of shift time
- **Update regularly** - Adjust when schedules change

### Machine assignments

- **One shift per machine** (typically) - Simplifies analytics
- **Review periodically** - Ensure assignments stay current
- **Consider exceptions** - Some machines may run off-shift

### Analytics considerations

- **Allow ramp-up time** - First hour may differ from steady-state
- **Exclude changeovers** - Unless you want to track them
- **Compare like-to-like** - Same shift, different days

## Common configurations

### 24/7 operations

Three 8-hour shifts covering the full week:

| Shift | Time | Days |
|-------|------|------|
| A Shift | 6 AM - 2 PM | Mon-Sun |
| B Shift | 2 PM - 10 PM | Mon-Sun |
| C Shift | 10 PM - 6 AM | Mon-Sun |

### 5-day two-shift

Day and evening coverage on weekdays:

| Shift | Time | Days |
|-------|------|------|
| Day | 7 AM - 3:30 PM | Mon-Fri |
| Evening | 3:30 PM - 12 AM | Mon-Fri |

### Weekend differential

Additional shifts for weekend work:

| Shift | Time | Days |
|-------|------|------|
| Weekday Day | 6 AM - 2 PM | Mon-Fri |
| Weekend Day | 7 AM - 7 PM | Sat-Sun |
