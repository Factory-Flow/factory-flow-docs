Facilities represent the physical locations where your manufacturing operations take place. Use facilities to organize machines, filter analytics, and manage multi-site operations.

## Understanding facilities

A facility in Factory Flow typically represents:

- A factory or manufacturing plant
- A building or warehouse
- A geographic site or campus
- A department or production area

Facilities help you:

- **Organize machines** by physical location
- **Filter dashboards** to see site-specific data
- **Assign teams** to specific locations
- **Track performance** across multiple sites

## Creating a facility

To add a new facility:

1. Navigate to **Facilities** in the sidebar
2. Click **Create Facility**
3. Fill in the facility details:
   - **Name** - A unique, descriptive name (e.g., "Austin Plant", "Building A")
   - **Location** - Optional address or location identifier
   - **Description** - Notes about the facility's purpose or characteristics
4. Click **Create**

<Tip>
  Use consistent naming conventions across facilities to make filtering and reporting easier. Consider including location codes or abbreviations that your team already uses.
</Tip>

## Managing facilities

### Viewing facilities

The Facilities page shows all facilities in your organization:

- Facility name and description
- Number of machines assigned
- Creation and last update dates

Click on a facility to view its details and edit settings.

### Editing a facility

To update facility information:

1. Click on the facility to open its detail page
2. Click **Edit** or modify fields directly
3. Update the name, location, or description
4. Click **Save**

### Deleting a facility

To remove a facility:

1. Navigate to the facility's detail page
2. Click **Delete**
3. Confirm the deletion

<Warning>
  Deleting a facility does not delete the machines assigned to it. Those machines will become unassigned and can be reassigned to another facility.
</Warning>

## Assigning machines to facilities

Every machine in Factory Flow can be assigned to a facility. This helps you:

- Organize your machine inventory by location
- Filter the Machines list by facility
- Generate location-specific reports

### To assign a machine to a facility:

1. Navigate to **Machines**
2. Select the machine
3. In the **General** settings, select a **Facility** from the dropdown
4. Click **Save**

### Bulk operations

When creating multiple machines for the same location:

1. Create the facility first
2. When adding each machine, select the facility during creation
3. All machines will be automatically organized under that facility

## Filtering by facility

Use facility filters throughout Factory Flow:

### On the Machines page

1. Click the filter/dropdown control
2. Select one or more facilities
3. The list updates to show only machines in those facilities

### In Analytics

1. Navigate to **Analytics**
2. Use the facility filter to focus on specific locations
3. Compare performance across facilities

### In the Overview

The Overview dashboard can be filtered to show:
- Machines from specific facilities
- Status breakdowns per location
- Location-specific alerts

## Multi-site operations

For organizations with multiple manufacturing sites:

### Hierarchical organization

Consider organizing facilities to reflect your physical hierarchy:

| Facility Name | Purpose |
|---------------|---------|
| Austin Campus | Parent site identifier |
| Austin - Building A | Specific building |
| Austin - Building A - Line 1 | Production area |

Or use a flat structure with clear naming:

| Facility Name | Location |
|---------------|----------|
| Austin Plant | Austin, TX |
| Chicago Plant | Chicago, IL |
| Mexico Site | Monterrey, MX |

### Cross-facility reporting

Factory Flow analytics support:
- Comparing facilities side by side
- Aggregating metrics across all facilities
- Drilling down into specific locations

## Best practices

### Naming conventions

- **Be consistent** - Use the same format for all facilities
- **Be specific** - Include enough detail to identify the location
- **Consider abbreviations** - Use standard codes your team knows
- **Avoid duplicates** - Each facility needs a unique name

### Examples of good facility names

| Good | Why |
|------|-----|
| "Austin Plant - Main" | Clear location and identifier |
| "CHI-B2" | Short code following company standard |
| "Singapore Manufacturing Center" | Full descriptive name |

### Examples to avoid

| Avoid | Why |
|-------|-----|
| "Plant 1" | Too generic, unclear location |
| "New Facility" | Temporary name, not descriptive |
| "John's Area" | Personal reference, not professional |

### Organization tips

1. **Start with your physical structure** - Mirror your real-world organization
2. **Plan for growth** - Use a naming scheme that scales
3. **Document your conventions** - Share naming standards with your team
4. **Review regularly** - Update facility names when locations change
