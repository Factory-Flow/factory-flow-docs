Organizations are the top-level containers in Factory Flow. Each organization has its own isolated data, users, settings, and billing. This multi-tenant architecture ensures complete separation between different companies or business units.

## Understanding organizations

An organization in Factory Flow represents a single tenant—typically a company, division, or business unit. Everything you create in Factory Flow belongs to an organization:

- Facilities and machines
- Production lines and shifts
- Layouts and analytics data
- API tokens and integrations
- Users and their permissions

<Note>
  Users can belong to multiple organizations and easily switch between them. Each organization has its own subscription and billing.
</Note>

## Creating an organization

When you first sign up for Factory Flow, you'll create or join an organization as part of the onboarding process.

### To create a new organization:

1. Sign in to Factory Flow
2. If this is your first time, you'll see the onboarding screen
3. Click **Create Organization**
4. Enter your organization name
5. Select a subscription plan
6. Complete payment setup (if applicable)

Your organization is created immediately and you become the first Admin.

### Organization settings

After creation, configure your organization in **Organization Settings**:

- **Name** - The display name shown throughout Factory Flow
- **Timezone** - Used for shift scheduling and analytics reporting
- **Default preferences** - Configure organization-wide defaults

## Team structure

Organizations support teams of any size with role-based access control.

### User roles

Factory Flow has three permission levels:

| Role | Description | Key Permissions |
|------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Admin** | Full control | Manage members, billing, API tokens, delete organization |
| **Editor** | Create and modify | Add machines, lines, shifts, layouts; view analytics |
| **Viewer** | Read-only | View dashboards, analytics, and reports |

### Inviting team members

Admins can invite new members:

1. Go to **Organization Settings → Members**
2. Click **Invite Member**
3. Enter the email address
4. Select a role (Viewer, Editor, or Admin)
5. Click **Send Invitation**

The invited user receives an email with instructions to join. They can create a new account or sign in with an existing one.

### Managing invitations

From the Members page, you can:

- **Resend** - Send another invitation email
- **Revoke** - Cancel a pending invitation
- View invitation status and when it was sent

### Updating member roles

To change a member's role:

1. Go to **Organization Settings → Members**
2. Find the member in the list
3. Click the role dropdown
4. Select the new role

<Warning>
  Demoting yourself from Admin to a lower role may lock you out of administrative functions. Ensure another Admin exists before doing this.
</Warning>

### Removing members

To remove a member:

1. Go to **Organization Settings → Members**
2. Find the member
3. Click **Remove**
4. Confirm the removal

The member immediately loses access to the organization.

## Join requests

Users who find your organization through search can request to join.

### Handling join requests

When someone requests to join:

1. You'll see a notification in the Members section
2. Review the request (see the user's email and request date)
3. Click **Approve** or **Deny**

Approved users are added with the Viewer role by default. You can upgrade their role after they join.

## Switching organizations

If you belong to multiple organizations:

1. Click your organization name in the sidebar header
2. Select **Switch Organization**
3. Choose the organization to switch to

Or use the quick switcher in the navigation bar.

## Leaving an organization

To leave an organization you're a member of:

1. Go to **Organization Settings → Members**
2. Find your name in the list
3. Click **Leave Organization**
4. Confirm your decision

<Warning>
  If you're the last member of an organization, leaving will permanently delete the organization and all its data.
</Warning>

## Deleting an organization

Organizations can only be deleted when there's one member remaining (the owner). To delete:

1. Ensure you're the only member (remove all others first)
2. Go to **Organization Settings**
3. Scroll to the **Danger Zone**
4. Click **Delete Organization**
5. Type the organization name to confirm
6. Click **Delete**

<Warning>
  This action is irreversible. All data including machines, analytics history, layouts, and configurations will be permanently deleted.
</Warning>

## Best practices

### Naming conventions

- Use clear, descriptive names that identify the business unit
- Consider including location or division (e.g., "Acme - Austin Plant")
- Avoid special characters that may cause issues in reports

### Role assignment

- **Viewers** - Floor operators who need to see dashboards
- **Editors** - Supervisors and engineers who configure the system
- **Admins** - IT staff and management who control access and billing

### Security recommendations

- Regularly audit member access in the Members section
- Remove former employees promptly
- Use SSO (Google, Microsoft, GitHub) for centralized authentication
- Limit the number of Admins to reduce risk
