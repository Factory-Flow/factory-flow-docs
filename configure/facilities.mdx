---
title: "Facilities"
description: "Represent your physical sites in Factory Flow for location-based analytics and organization."
---

Facilities represent the physical locations where your production happens — a plant, a warehouse, a building, or any other named site. Organizing your machines and lines under facilities makes it easy to roll up analytics by location and compare performance across sites.

## The Facilities List

Navigate to **Configure → Facilities** to see all registered facilities. The list shows each facility's name, location, and how many machines and lines are assigned to it.

</Frame>

---

## Creating a Facility

Click **New Facility** and fill in the details:

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Name** | The facility's name (e.g. "Chicago Plant" or "Site B — East Wing"). |
| **Location** | Optional. A city, address, or site code to help identify the physical location. |
| **Description** | Optional. A note about what is produced or operated at this facility. |

Save the facility. You can now assign lines and machines to it when creating or editing them.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/facilities-new.png" alt="New Facility form with name, location, and description fields" />
</Frame>

---

## Facility Analytics

With lines and machines assigned, navigate to **Monitor → Analytics** and select a facility from the dropdown. Facility analytics aggregate all machines and lines at the site into a single view, making it easy to see overall site performance at a glance.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/facilities-analytics.png" alt="Facility-level analytics view showing rolled-up KPIs across all lines and machines at the site" />
</Frame>

<Tip>
  If your organization has multiple facilities, the facility-level analytics view is the fastest way to compare site-to-site performance in a single screen.
</Tip>
